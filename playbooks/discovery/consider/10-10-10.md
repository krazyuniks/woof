# 10/10/10

Apply the 10/10/10 rule to a decision raised by the spark or the emerging
direction. Ask how the decision will look in 10 minutes, 10 months, and 10
years.

## Process

1. State the decision clearly, with its options.
2. For each option, evaluate the impact at 10 minutes (immediate reaction), 10
   months (medium-term consequences), and 10 years (long-term impact).
3. Identify where the short-term and long-term views conflict.
4. Recommend an option, weighted toward the longer horizons.

## Output

Write the artefact as `10-10-10.md` into the `.woof/epics/E<N>/discovery/thinking/` bucket directory declared in the
graph-owned input. Use this shape:

```
## 10/10/10: <decision>

### Decision
<what is being chosen between>

### Option <n>
- 10 minutes: <immediate consequence>
- 10 months: <medium-term reality>
- 10 years: <long-term impact>

### Time conflicts
<where short-term pain leads to long-term gain, or the reverse>

### Recommendation
<which option, weighted toward the longer horizons>
```

## Success criteria

- Distinguishes temporary discomfort from lasting regret.
- Reveals when short-term thinking is hijacking the decision.
- Makes long-term consequences concrete.
- Clarifies what actually matters over time.
