# Pareto Principle

Apply the Pareto principle to the spark and the emerging direction. Identify the
vital few factors (roughly 20%) that drive most of the result (roughly 80%),
cutting through noise to what actually matters.

## Process

1. Identify the factors, options, tasks, or considerations in scope.
2. Estimate the relative impact of each on the desired outcome.
3. Rank them by impact, highest to lowest.
4. Find the cutoff where roughly 20% of factors account for roughly 80% of
   impact.
5. Present the vital few with specific actions, and note what to deprioritise.

## Output

Write the artefact as `pareto.md` into the `discovery/thinking/` bucket directory declared in the
graph-owned input. Use this shape:

```
## Pareto Analysis: <scope>

### Vital few (focus here)
- <factor>: <why it matters, specific action>

### Trivial many (deprioritise)
- <what can be deferred or ignored>

### Bottom line
<one sentence on where to focus effort for maximum results>
```

## Success criteria

- Clearly separates high-impact from low-impact factors.
- Gives specific, actionable recommendations for the vital few.
- Explains why each vital factor matters.
- Gives clear direction on what to ignore or defer.
